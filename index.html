Keep coding, testing, building, and shipping, even if Github goes down.

Gitproxy continuously mirrors your code and dependencies and transparently takes over if your git hosting provider is unavailable.

Works with these fine (yet fallible) providers

Gitproxy works by proxying all of your git commands to your normal git host (when everything is going well.) If their service is down, gitproxy steps in transparently to serve those repositories from our mirrors.

It's like a git backup solution on steroids, allowing you to keep working, building, testing, and deploying your service even when you can't push or pull from Github or Gitlab. When they come back up, gitproxy steps out of the way automatically once your normal git host is ready to work.

Ridiculously over-engineered to make sure you can still work when everything else is broken.

3 cloud providers

Servers on three different cloud providers work in concert to provide the service. Even if AWS and GCP both go down at the same time, gitproxy will remain up.

6 availability zones with geo-replication

Every snapshot of your git repos and dependencies is replicated to six independent data centers on two sides of the continent.

Serverless autoscaling with auto-failover

Autoscaling and failover ensures that even during high traffic spikes due to flaky infrastructure, your requests will get through.

Continuously mirrors not only your code, but all your dependencies too

Every push is continuously mirrored on our infrastructure. We figure out what dependencies you need to build and back those up too.

And it only costs about $4 per month for your whole team

Gitproxy is like car insurance. 99% of the time, we aren't doing anything except mirroring your repos and dependencies, keeping our costs low.

When your repo host goes down, we incur a short-term spike in bandwidth costs to serve that data, but over the long term, we can afford to provide the service at a fraction of what it might cost Github to achieve the same reliability.

We do just one thing - store your bits in as many places as necessary and with as many service points as possible so you can get them when you need them. 100% money-back guaranteed.
